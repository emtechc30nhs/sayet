SPACGA (SAYET PREMIUM ARABICA COFFEE GROWERS ASSOCIATION)

    Before Arabica coffee farming is just a backyard traditionand a source of "tinapong" of coffee grounds for the residents. Later on people began to sell their coffee beans in Baguio City  but in  small quantities. This system continued for years until farmers realized the demand of Arabica coffee.

    Years later  the Arabica industry grow. With the help of Mr. and Mrs. Young who created a partnership among the farmers, they departed some knowledge about Arabica Coffee farming. Indeed the Arabica industry grow one step more, a lot of farmers have gain knowledge on how to improve their harvest.

    Like the seasons, buyers come and go. Competition among buyers are present and a new name made it on the picture. The Rocky Mountains made it to Atok and even  got their warehouse at Camp 30. However they did last , after years they dissapered from Atok.

    Then there came two names who brought a big change in the industry, they Mr. Abellion and Mr. Reyes. They have done so much for the improvement of the industry especially int the giving of knowledge on to improve the harvest and they also introduce the proper sorting of coffee. They have also a part that the SPACGA is formed.

    SPACGA now is a cooperative that enables farmers to come together to improve the industry and improve their income.

WHAT IS REALLY ARABICA COFFEE FARMING?

    According to the Arabica farmers, Arabica farming is a years of investment, hardwork, sweat and determination. They have said that before the coffee reach the cup it takes a long and hard process. From just planting the seedlings it takes years before it will bear fruit and it also comes with annual cleaning of roots.

    Harvesting is also not an easy part, farmers must distinguished the ripe ones from the half ripe ones and must also be careful not to destroy the tree. The size of the beans add hardship to harvesting and also the height of the tree. After harvesting another long and hard process awaits. From peeling the beans, washing, drying, peeling again and sorting. It is not easy.

    Most people say that being an Arabica coffee farmer is no worth it because it takes a lot of work plus you only harvest once in a year. But it is not to these farmers, they choose to take the risk and with hardwork they have succeed. To most parent, Arabica farming is the key why they have finished their children to school. It is also the one responsible for feeding families.

    THESE FARMERS ARE EXTRAORDINANRY NOT JUST FOR THEIR CONTRIBUTIONS IN THE INDUSTRY BUT FOR THEIR LOVE IN WHAT THEY ARE DOING.
